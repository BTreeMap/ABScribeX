William Shakespeare (1564-1616) was an English playwright, poet, and actor. He is widely regarded as the greatest writer in the English language and the world's pre-eminent dramatist.

Shakespeare's works include 39 plays, 154 sonnets, and two long narrative poems. His plays have been translated into every major living language and are performed more often than those of any other playwright.

Some of his most famous works include "Romeo and Juliet," "Hamlet," "Othello," "King Lear," and "Macbeth." Shakespeare's influence on the English language and literature is profound and enduring.
